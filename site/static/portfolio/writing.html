Writing Samples

A few of my favorite articles about web technologies and design. You can find links to all of my writing here.

“Hugo Templates for WordPress Designers” (Web, PDF)

Source: Treehouse Blog
Technologies: HTML, code snippets, WordPress, screenshots
Tone: Informational, informal
Purpose: Tutorial, brand awareness
Audience: Intermediate-senior web developers

I wrote this blog post for the Treehouse blog after learning a lot about Go and Hugo and using them for the Balsamiq documentation site.

I thought that it could be useful to share the similarities and differences between Hugo and Wordpress templates for people who were considering making the jump.

For this article, I was both the author and SME. I added the article and images to Wordpress as a guest author, and it underwent light editing by the Treehouse blog editor.

“Using Wireframes with Agile User Stories” (Web, PDF)

Source: Balsamiq Wireframing Academy
Technologies: Markdown, GitHub, custom JavaScript static site, wireframes
Tone: Informal, helpful, professional
Purpose: Tutorial, brand awareness
Audience: Product Managers, Business Analysts, and other Agile development team members

One of my first articles for Balsamiq, this first-person account describes what I learned in a previous role where I was a designer and user story writer.

It covers three steps to writing better user stories that maximize your impact without requiring you to develop complex prototypes or explain every implementation detail.

I was both the author and SME. I added the article and images to Balsamiq's custom static site by writing in Markdown and adding the files via GitHub.

“What is UI Design” (Web, PDF)

Source: Balsamiq Wireframing Academy
Technologies: Markdown, GitHub, custom JavaScript static site, wireframes, screenshots, image editing
Tone: Informal, authoritative, informational
Purpose: Education, brand awareness
Audience: Product Managers, Business Analysts, and other non-designers who participate in the software development process.

There are many articles out there explaining what UI design is but many of them dive into the details too soon or assume a high level of design knowledge already.

Instead, this article explains the basics of UI design by viewing different layers of UI design through the lens of a user interface designer.

I was both the author and SME. I added the article and images to Balsamiq's custom static site by writing in Markdown and adding the files via GitHub.
