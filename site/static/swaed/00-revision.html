0.2 Internal vs. External Guideline Details (reminder from 4.0)

In September, 2007, the Web Application Guidelines v4.0 became available to an external audience available on the Sun Developer Network. The two primary reasons for doing this were:

    * To support the Woodstock components that have already been open sourced
    * To move in the direction of open source and community involvement

The guidelines have not been made to sound like they were originally intended for an external audience. Instead, they are being offered as Sun guidelines available for external folks to look at and use as appropriate for their needs.

In order to make the guidelines suitable for an external audience, a number of edits were made. Except for a few chapters, the external version of the guidelines and the internal version of the guidelines are the same so the edits made to externalize the guidelines affect both internal and external readers.

    * The guidelines files now live in two locations (an internal location, and an external location).
    * Chapter 1, this revision history, and a couple of appendix files differ in the two locations, but all other chapters are the same.
    * Future updates to the guidelines will be uploaded to the two locations.

The main types of edits are listed below:

    * The name of the guidelines is now preceded with 'Sun'.
    * Each chapter has been "scrubbed" to remove references to internal sites and groups.
       (e.g. UIRB, Legal, Branding, SWAED & SWAED approvals, etc.)
    * Chapter 1 (internal) now has all the references to the Sun internal sites and groups.

    * References to swaed@sun.com and swaed-core@sun.com have been replaced with a Send Feedback link which takes you to a form.
       People can use this form to offer feedback that we can then consider including in the guidelines.
    * Search criteria is now entered on a separate page. This allows:
          - the external guidelines to be searched from the external location.
          - the internal guidelines (and older guidelines and meeting minutes) to be searched from the internal location.
